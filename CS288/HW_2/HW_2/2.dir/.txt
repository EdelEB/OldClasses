From today's featured article

   [23]Interstate 70 in Colorado

   [24]Interstate 70 passes through Colorado on its route between its
   western terminus at [25]Cove Fort, Utah, and its eastern at
   [26]Baltimore, Maryland. In western Colorado, the highway connects the
   metropolitan areas of [27]Grand Junction and [28]Denver via a route
   through the [29]Rocky Mountains. Bicycles are allowed on those
   stretches of I-70 in the Rockies where no other through route exists.
   The [30]United States Department of Transportation (USDOT) lists the
   construction of I-70 among the engineering marvels undertaken in the
   Interstate Highway system. The [31]Eisenhower Tunnel, with a maximum
   elevation of 11,158 feet (3,401 m) and length of 1.7 miles (2.7 km), is
   the longest mountain tunnel and highest point along the [32]Interstate
   Highway System. The portion through [33]Glenwood Canyon was completed
   on October 14, 1992, one of the final pieces of the Interstate Highway
   System to open to traffic, and one of the most expensive rural highways
   per mile built in the United States. ([34]Full article...)

From today's featured list

   [123]Niven in Enchantment (1948)
   Niven in [124]Enchantment (1948)

   British actor [125]David Niven's [126]entertainment career began in
   1932, where he was an uncredited extra in [127]There Goes the Bride.
   Niven went on to appear in nearly a hundred films. His film credits
   include critical successes such as [128]A Matter of Life and Death
   (1946), [129]The Bishop's Wife (1947), [130]Carrington V.C. (1954), and
   [131]Around the World in 80 Days (1956). Niven received two [132]Golden
   Globe Awards for his performances in [133]The Moon Is Blue (1953) and
   [134]Separate Tables (1958); the latter performance also earned him the
   [135]Academy Award for Best Actor. Niven also starred in the American
   television anthology series [136]Four Star Playhouse (1952–1956), which
   earned him an [137]Emmy nomination at the [138]7th Primetime Emmy
   Awards. ([139]Full list...)

Today's featured picture

   [145]Pula Arena

   This is a panoramic view of the interior of the [146]Pula Arena, a
   [147]Roman amphitheatre in [148]Pula, Croatia. Constructed between
   27 BC and AD 86, it is among the six largest surviving Roman arenas in
   the world, and is the best-preserved ancient monument in the country.
   The amphitheatre appears on the Croatian ten-[149]kuna banknote.
